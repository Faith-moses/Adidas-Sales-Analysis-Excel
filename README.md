# Adidas Sales Data Analysis Project (Excel Dashboard)

## Introduction

This project presents a detailed analysis of Adidas sales data, carried out as part of a Data Analysis training program with beTechified.

The goal of this project is to understand how Adidas can use its sales data to improve business performance, increase revenue, and make better strategic decisions.

This analysis focuses on identifying key trends across products, regions, retailers, and sales channels.

---

## Problem Statement

Adidas generates large volumes of sales data but may not fully utilize this data to drive decision-making.

The key questions addressed in this project include:

* Which product categories generate the most revenue?
* Which regions perform best?
* Which retailers contribute the most to sales?
* Which sales channels are most effective?
* Where are the opportunities for improvement?

---

This project was completed using:

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains Adidas sales records with the following key fields:

* Retailer
* Retailer ID
* Invoice date
* Region
* State
* City
* Product
* Price per unit
* Units sold
* Total Sales
* Operating profit
* Operating margin
* Sales method (In-store, Outlet, Online)

The dataset covers sales performance across different regions and time periods, with total revenue exceeding $899 million.

---

## Data Cleaning & Preparation

Before analysis, the dataset was cleaned and prepared to ensure accuracy:

* No duplicate entries
* No missing values
* Ensured correct data formats for numerical and date fields

Note: While reviewing the dataset, we observed that sales values appeared higher than expected when compared with price per unit and quantity. However, this pattern was consistent across the dataset, suggesting a possible scaling factor rather than random errors. Therefore, the data was not modified to avoid introducing bias.

## Dashboard

An interactive dashboard was created in Excel using pivot tables and slicers to visualize key insights and allow filtering by:

* Region

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs were identified:

* Total Revenue: $899.9 million
* Operating Profit: $332.1 million
* Profit Margin: 42%
* Total Units Sold: Over 2.4 million units

These indicators show that Adidas is generating strong revenue and maintaining healthy profitability.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Product Performance

* Men’s Street Footwear is the top-performing category
* Women’s Athletic Footwear is the lowest-performing category

👉 This suggests strong demand for certain product segments and opportunities to improve weaker categories.

---

### 2. Regional Performance

* The West region generates the highest revenue
* Northeast also shows strong performance

👉 Indicates stronger market demand in these regions.

---

### 3. Retailer Performance

* West Gear is the top-performing retailer
* Foot Locker also contributes significantly

👉 Strong partnerships drive higher revenue.

---

### 4. Sales Trend

* Significant growth from 2020 to 2021

👉 Indicates business growth and market recovery.

---

### 5. Sales Method

* In-store sales dominate
* Online sales are the lowest but have growth potential

👉 Shows opportunity for digital expansion.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

### 1. Product Strategy

Adidas should focus on high-performing products such as Men’s Street Footwear while improving underperforming categories like Women’s Athletic Footwear through better marketing and repositioning.

---

### 2. Retailer Strategy

Strengthen partnerships with top retailers such as West Gear and Foot Locker, as they contribute significantly to overall revenue.

---

### 3. Regional Strategy

Increase investment in high-performing regions like the West and Northeast while implementing targeted strategies to improve performance in weaker regions.

---

### 4. Sales Channel Optimization

Invest more in online and outlet sales channels to capture future growth and improve customer reach.

---

### 5. Demand Forecasting

Use historical sales data to improve inventory planning and reduce stock shortages or overstocking.
